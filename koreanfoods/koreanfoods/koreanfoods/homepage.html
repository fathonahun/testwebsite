Popular Korean Food

Tteokbokki

Tteokbokki is a popular Korean street food made from chewy rice cakes (tteok) cooked in a spicy, sweet, and savory sauce typically made with gochujang (Korean chili paste). It often includes additional ingredients like fish cakes, boiled eggs, and scallions.

Rp40.000

Jjampong

Jjampong is a popular Korean-Chinese noodle soup known for its spicy, flavorful broth. It typically contains a variety of seafood such as squid, shrimp, and mussels, along with vegetables like cabbage, onions, and carrots. Pork or beef is often added for extra depth of flavor. The soup is seasoned with gochugaru (Korean red chili flakes) and other spices, creating a rich, spicy taste. Served with thick wheat noodles, jjampong is a hearty and satisfying dish commonly found in Korean-Chinese restaurants.

Rp60.000

Jjajangmyeon

Jjajangmyeon is a Korean-Chinese noodle dish topped with a thick, savory black bean sauce. The sauce is made from chunjang (a salty black soybean paste), diced pork or beef, and vegetables such as onions, zucchini, and potatoes. The dish is garnished with cucumber strips or peas and served over thick wheat noodles. Jjajangmyeon is a beloved comfort food in Korea, often enjoyed during casual meals and celebrations.

Rp50.000

Kimchi

Kimchi is a traditional Korean side dish made from fermented vegetables, most commonly Napa cabbage and Korean radishes, seasoned with a variety of spices and flavorings like garlic, ginger, red chili pepper flakes, and salted seafood. The mixture is left to ferment for a period of time, allowing the flavors to develop and creating its distinctive tangy and spicy taste. Kimchi is a staple in Korean cuisine, enjoyed on its own as a side dish, or used in various dishes such as kimchi fried rice, kimchi stew (kimchi jjigae), and kimchi pancakes (kimchijeon).

Rp15.000

Bulgogi

Bulgogi is a popular Korean dish made from thinly sliced beef that is marinated in a mixture of soy sauce, sugar, sesame oil, garlic, and other seasonings. The marinated beef is then grilled or stir-fried, resulting in a flavorful and tender dish. Bulgogi is often served with rice, lettuce wraps, and various side dishes (banchan). It is known for its sweet and savory taste and is a staple in Korean cuisine, enjoyed both at home and in restaurants.

Rp200.000

Ganjang Gejang

Ganjang Gejang is a traditional Korean dish consisting of raw crabs marinated in soy sauce. The crabs are typically cleaned and then soaked in a flavorful marinade made from soy sauce, garlic, ginger, sugar, and sometimes additional ingredients like chili peppers and sesame oil. This marinating process not only preserves the crabs but also infuses them with a rich, savory flavor. Ganjang Gejang is often referred to as "rice thief" because its salty and umami-rich taste pairs exceptionally well with steamed rice.

Rp450.000

Gyeranjjim

Gyeranjjim is a Korean steamed egg dish. It's made by whisking eggs and then gently cooking them with water or broth, often in a hot stone bowl. The dish is typically seasoned with ingredients like salt, green onions, and sesame oil. The result is a light, fluffy, and savory custard-like texture. Gyeranjjim is commonly served as a side dish (banchan) in Korean meals and is appreciated for its simplicity and comforting taste.

Rp80.000

Naengmyeon

Naengmyeon is a Korean cold noodle dish typically enjoyed in the summer. It features long, thin noodles made from buckwheat or sweet potatoes, served in a cold, tangy broth or mixed with a spicy sauce. The dish is often garnished with slices of cold beef, cucumber, pear, and a boiled egg. There are two main varieties: mul naengmyeon (with broth) and bibim naengmyeon (with spicy sauce). Naengmyeon is known for its refreshing taste and chewy texture.

Rp70.000

Samgyetang

Samgyetang is a traditional Korean chicken soup made with a whole young chicken stuffed with glutinous rice, ginseng, garlic, jujube fruits, and other medicinal herbs. The dish is simmered for hours until the chicken is tender and infused with the flavors of the ingredients. Samgyetang is typically enjoyed during the hot summer months in Korea, as it is believed to have nourishing and revitalizing properties that help to combat the heat and restore energy. It's served piping hot and often accompanied by a bowl of salt and ground black pepper for seasoning to taste.

Rp140.000

Kimchi Jjigae

Kimchi jjigae is a popular Korean stew made with kimchi, tofu, pork, or other proteins, and various vegetables. The base of the stew is typically fermented kimchi, which gives the dish its characteristic tangy and spicy flavor. Additional ingredients such as pork belly, tofu, onions, garlic, and green onions are often added to enhance the taste. The stew is simmered until the flavors meld together, creating a rich and comforting dish that is often enjoyed with a bowl of steamed rice. Kimchi jjigae is a beloved comfort food in Korean cuisine, especially during cold weather.

Rp100.000

Samgyeopsal

Samgyeopsal is a popular Korean dish consisting of thick slices of pork belly grilled at the table. The name "samgyeopsal" literally translates to "three-layered flesh," referring to the layers of meat, fat, and skin in pork belly. The pork slices are usually cooked over a charcoal or gas grill until crispy on the outside and tender on the inside. Samgyeopsal is typically served with a variety of accompaniments such as lettuce leaves, ssamjang (spicy dipping sauce), sliced garlic, fresh vegetables like green onions and peppers, and various kimchi. It's a beloved dish in Korean cuisine, often enjoyed during social gatherings with friends and family.

Rp200.000

Korean Fried Chicken

Korean fried chicken, often abbreviated as KFC, is a popular dish in Korean cuisine known for its crispy, crunchy texture and flavorful seasoning. Unlike traditional American fried chicken, Korean fried chicken is typically double-fried, resulting in an extra crispy coating while keeping the meat inside juicy and tender. It is often coated in a variety of sauces or seasonings, such as spicy gochujang (Korean chili paste), sweet soy garlic, or honey butter. Korean fried chicken is commonly served as an appetizer, snack, or main course in Korean restaurants, and it has gained popularity worldwide for its delicious taste and addictive crunch.

Rp100.000

Kimbab

Kimbap, also spelled gimbap, is a popular Korean dish made from steamed white rice and various fillings, all rolled in dried seaweed (nori) and sliced into bite-sized pieces. The fillings can vary widely and often include ingredients such as vegetables like carrots, cucumbers, spinach, and pickled radish, as well as proteins like beef, tuna, or egg omelet. The rolls are often served with dipping sauce or seasoned sesame oil for added flavor. Kimbap is a convenient and portable snack or meal in Korean cuisine, enjoyed for its versatility, flavor, and ease of consumption.

Rp25.000

Bungeopppang

Bungeoppang, also known as "fish-shaped bread" or "Taiyaki" in Japanese, is a popular Korean street food snack. It consists of a fish-shaped pastry made from a batter similar to pancake or waffle batter, which is poured into a fish-shaped mold and filled with sweet or savory fillings. Traditional fillings include sweet red bean paste (pat), custard, chocolate, or cheese. The pastry is then cooked until golden brown and crispy on the outside while remaining soft and fluffy on the inside. Bungeoppang is often enjoyed hot off the grill, especially during colder months, and it's a favorite treat among Koreans of all ages.

Rp15.000

Chocolate Bingsu

Chocolate bingsu is a delicious variation of the traditional Korean shaved ice dessert, bingsu. It features finely shaved ice topped with rich chocolate syrup, chocolate shavings, chocolate sauce, and sometimes additional toppings like chocolate cookies, brownie pieces, or chocolate ice cream. The combination of creamy chocolate flavors with the light and fluffy texture of the shaved ice makes chocolate bingsu a decadent and indulgent treat, especially popular among chocolate lovers. It's a delightful dessert enjoyed in Korea and beyond, particularly during hot weather as a refreshing and satisfying sweet treat.

Rp40.000

Hotteok

Hotteok is a popular Korean street food and sweet pancake that is enjoyed especially during the colder months. It is made from a dough of wheat flour, water, milk, sugar, and yeast, which is then filled with a sweet mixture typically consisting of brown sugar, honey, chopped peanuts, and cinnamon. The filled dough is then flattened into a disc shape and fried until golden brown and crispy on the outside, while the inside remains soft and chewy with the melted filling. Hotteok is often served piping hot and is a beloved treat for its warm, sweet, and comforting flavors.

Rp15.000
